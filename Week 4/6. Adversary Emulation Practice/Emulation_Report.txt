Adversary emulation was conducted using a simulated spearphishing attack mapped to MITRE ATT&CK technique T1566.

A phishing email containing a malicious attachment was generated, and Wazuh successfully detected the suspicious activity.

The alert indicated the presence of a potentially harmful file, demonstrating effective detection capabilities.

However, the simulation highlights the need for enhanced email filtering and user awareness to prevent initial access.

While detection mechanisms were effective, proactive prevention measures should be improved.

This exercise confirms that SOC monitoring tools can identify phishing attempts but require continuous tuning to reduce false positives and improve response efficiency.
